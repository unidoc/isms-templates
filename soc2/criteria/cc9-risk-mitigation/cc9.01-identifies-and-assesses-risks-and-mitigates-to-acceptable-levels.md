# CC9.1: Risk identification, assessment, and mitigation

## Purpose

The entity identifies, selects, and develops risk mitigation activities for risks arising
from potential business disruptions.

## Description

The organization develops risk mitigation strategies for identified risks, including risk
acceptance, avoidance, transfer (insurance, contracts), and reduction (controls). Risk
mitigation activities are proportional to the risk level and the entity's risk appetite.
Business continuity and disaster recovery plans address risks from potential disruptions.

## Key Considerations

- Risk mitigation strategies are defined for each significant risk
- Risk acceptance decisions are documented and approved by management
- Insurance coverage is evaluated for risk transfer
- Business continuity and disaster recovery plans exist
- Recovery time and recovery point objectives are defined
- Plans are tested at least annually
