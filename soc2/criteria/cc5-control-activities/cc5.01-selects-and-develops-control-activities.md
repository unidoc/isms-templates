# CC5.1: Selects and develops control activities

## Purpose

The entity selects and develops control activities that contribute to the mitigation of
risks to the achievement of objectives to acceptable levels.

## Description

The organization designs and implements control activities that address identified risks.
Controls are selected based on the nature of the risk, the cost-benefit of the control,
and the entity's risk appetite. Control activities include approvals, authorizations,
verifications, reconciliations, reviews of performance, and segregation of duties.

## Key Considerations

- Controls are designed to mitigate identified risks
- Mix of preventive and detective controls
- Automated controls are preferred where feasible
- Controls are documented with clear ownership
- Cost-benefit analysis informs control selection
